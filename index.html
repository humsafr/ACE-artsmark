Future service model

Three connected parts, one framework

Artsmark becomes a service made up of three connected parts. Schools can enter where they need to, move between them over time, or use one part without committing to the others.

One Artsmark framework

Shared criteria for audit, strengthening, evidence, assessment, feedback and examples.

Change strategy

Sequenced learning

The transition strategy is to avoid building all three parts at once. The next year can be used to sequence them carefully, so each strand helps test and strengthen the others.

We start with the award because this is the clearest source of value for schools and the strongest hook into Artsmark. The immediate priority is to simplify and test a clearer award process with schools that already understand Artsmark, before opening it up to newer schools.

In parallel, we introduce a lighter-touch self-audit offer for newly registered schools. Practice share sessions can launch first because they are visible, valuable and relatively low-risk. They also support both other strands: schools using self-audit can learn from real examples, and schools preparing for award can see what strong practice looks like.
